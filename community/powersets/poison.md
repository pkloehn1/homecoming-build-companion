## Summary

- A long-form guide arguing that Poison is the most aggressive debuff set in the game and is best played in melee range to leverage Venomous Gas; passive play with Poison is described as always underwhelming, and the OP recommends the set primarily on Defenders (for correctly-scaled debuff values and ATO synergy) and Controllers (for innate mez/mitigation and the highest -special modifier on Weaken), and against using it on Corruptors or Masterminds.
- Per-power analysis covers all nine Poison powers, with strong recommendations to take Envenom, Weaken, Poison Trap and Venomous Gas as the four cornerstones; Alkaloid, Antidote, Neurotoxic Breath are largely skippable on Defenders, and Elixir of Life is presented as a Vengeance-bait gimmick rather than a real rez. Paralytic Poison is good while leveling and a passable proc holder at 50.
- Mechanics deep-dive: Envenom gives the largest single-application -res in the game and stacks main+AoE debuffs to about -60% res when alternating targets; Weaken's -damage scales further when combined with -resistance debuffs and floors enemy damage near -90% in many fights, but the purple patch at +3 and resistance-resistance can severely cut the effective debuff (Dra'Gon used as a worked example); Poison Trap procs unusually well but has several mechanical bugs (only its slotted accuracy applies, the 3rd/4th proc rolls auto-fail, and the -recovery hardly fires).
- Build-synergy guidance: mez protection is Poison's biggest hole (recommended fixes: P2W defense amplifier while leveling, Rune of Protection, Melee Hybrid, Clarion, or stacking ranged + melee defense); cones conflict with the in-melee playstyle so PBAoE/TAoE secondaries are preferred; healing should come from procs (Panacea/Power Transfer in Health/Stamina, the Defender ATO procs in 10s recharge attacks, Entomb proc in Paralytic Poison or Dominate); recommended pools are Hasten, Leadership, Combat Jumping, Sorcery (for Rune of Protection); recommended epics in order are Psychic (Dominate + World of Confusion), Dark (perma Soul Drain), and Mu (endurance solution).
- The author's own build is a Poison/Fire Defender with Psychic Mastery, T4 Musculature Radial alpha and T4 Melee Core hybrid, picking resistance plus -damage rather than soft-capped defense and cycling Rune of Protection with Melee Hybrid for mez/regen uptime. Section 6 reports consistent ~1:50 pylon times (~475 DPS) with Assault Radial and ~2:15 (~410 DPS) with Melee Core, and discusses solo TF and Giant Monster strategy under a self-imposed no-inspiration / no-temp / no-Lore rule.

## Verbatim excerpt

### Post 1 — Section: Sections 1–5 (Frosticus, 8 April 2020)

The OP frames Poison as "the most aggressive debuff set in the game" and "the kinetics of debuffing." Played passively the results are described as always underwhelming; the set requires melee range because the capstone Venomous Gas is a PBAoE aura that bundles several debuffs together. The author warns that players who arrive expecting a Radiation-style experience often struggle, but those who enjoyed melee-oriented Blasters or Dominators tend to pick it up well. The thesis is summarized as "you either poison the enemy so they are weaker than you, or you die."

A table of contents lists Sections 1–7 (AT overview, per-power overview, deep-dive on key mechanics, build synergies, the author's build, fun stuff/pylon/TF/GM, and "the future").

**Section 1 — AT choice.** Poison is available to four ATs. Strong recommendation for Controllers and Defenders for very different reasons; against Corruptors and Masterminds. Corruptors' debuff values and proc damage are noticeably lower than Defenders' once AT scaling is applied — the OP says "defs are noticeably more potent with poison" — but the Corruptor ATO's health/end and damage-proc still work fine. Masterminds get acceptable debuff values but small AoE radii, and the t9 (Venomous Gas) has bad uptime on MMs; if MMs got the other ATs' t9 they could function as a Poison-Trap-tankermind. Controllers are praised because their innate mitigation compensates for Poison's lack of self-defense, the set adds AoE damage and quick AoE containment via stacked holds, and Controllers get "by far the best modifier on the -special aspect of weaken" — described as nearly twice the Corruptor value. Defenders get correctly-scaled debuffs across the board and ATO procs that supply consistent absorb/heal — the OP calls this "the quintessential poison experience" and notes the rest of the guide is Defender-focused.

**Section 2 — per-power overview.** Strengths: unparalleled speed of swinging a fight by drastically weakening foes. Weaknesses: no mez protection, no sustain, requires melee range.

- _Alkaloid_ — slow projectile, single-target ally heal. Skippable on Defenders unless duo/trio; otherwise the OP "just view[s] you as vengeance bait." Slot a heal set, or a Preventative Medicine proc if leaving it unslotted.
- _Envenom_ — second-best power in the set, available level 1, ~1.33s cast / 12s recharge. Main target: -40% res, -37.5% def, plus minor -regen / -heal. 8ft AoE: half values. Recommended slotting: accuracy, Achilles, then two damage procs.
- _Weaken_ — extremely unique. Spammable, with an 8ft AoE half-debuff. Main target: -37.5% damage, -18.75% to-hit, -74.5% special (described as a "reverse powerboost"). Slotting: Cloud Senses fits well.
- _Neurotoxic Breath_ — narrow cone, conflicts with the melee Venomous Gas playstyle. Decent while leveling; once Venomous Gas is online "this will sit in your tray collecting dust." Optional smashing-damage proc to visualize hits.
- _Elixir of Life_ — gives the rez target ~100% recharge, 50% damage and unlimited end for 90 seconds, then a mag-1000 hold (toggle drop) plus puking and debuffs. Played as a Vengeance-bait gag — "Best used on scrappers as their swords tend to be sharper than they are."
- _Antidote_ — single-target ally mez protection, slow/-rech protection, minor cold/toxic res. A spot for unique Res IOs if Fighting is skipped.
- _Paralytic Poison_ — slow single-target hold; rolls procs well thanks to abundant -res.
- _Poison Trap_ (PPT) — distinct from Traps' Poison Trap (TPT); does a small AoE hold, light toxic dot, and takes both Hold and PBAoE IOs. Slot "as many procs as your build can support."
- _Venomous Gas_ — auto-hit PBAoE aura: 25% -res, 12.5% to-hit, 18.75% -dam, 12.5% -def. The OP describes this as the power that probably "fixed" Poison when it was ported off MMs. Slotting: Achilles, To-Hit Debuff/End.

**Section 3 — deep-dive mechanics.**

- _Envenom_ is called the most powerful single-application -res power in the game; alternating targets stacks main + AoE for ~-60% res. The -regen at 50% is unimpressive but stacks; stacking 3–4 applications can blunt a GM's regen. The -heal occasionally matters when paired with Weaken on a healer.
- _Weaken_ is highlighted because the -damage debuff is amplified by -resistance, so 37.5% -dam combined with 65% -res (Envenom + Venomous Gas) effectively becomes 60% -dam. Poison can routinely floor enemies at the -90% damage cap. -damage stacks multiplicatively with personal resistance (90% -dam + 50% res = ~5% incoming damage, etc). Two big spoilers: the purple patch (+1 = 0.9, +2 = 0.8, +3 = 0.65, +4 = 0.48) hits debuffs hard at +3 and above, and "resistance resists damage debuffs" _and_ "resistance resists resistance debuffs." Worked example: Dra'Gon (60% fire resist) at +0 reduces a stacked 56.25% / 65% combo to ~31.85% effective -dam vs a normal enemy's 92.81% (capped at 90%); at +3 the same combo lands at ~19.37% vs ~52.13% on a normal enemy. The author cautions to watch for AVs popping resistance powers (e.g. Unstoppable). Weaken's -special is also called out as making things like Ghost Widow's mag-100 hold and Silver Mantis's defense debuffs much shorter / much weaker.
- _Poison Trap_ (PPT) detonates very fast on enemy contact into a ~7-second AoE hold plus a toxic DoT cloud that periodically re-rolls procs. Initial detonation rolls 3.5 ppm procs at ~40–50%, with later cloud rolls at lower probability. Documented issues: it only uses accuracy slotted directly into the power (global accuracy, to-hit buffs and yellow inspirations don't apply, but Venomous Gas's to-hit debuff helps); the 3rd and 4th proc rolls are set up to auto-fail (>95 always); the power can occasionally "critical" double-hit with PBAoE IOs; and the -recovery hardly ever applies and lasts ~1 second when it does. Despite the bugs, the OP says PPT can frequently exceed 100% proc rate over its lifetime and "can often defeat most of the minions on its own."

**Section 4 — synergies.**

- _Mez protection_ is named the biggest weakness because Venomous Gas's toggle drop is often lethal. Options listed: teaming, inspirations (the OP avoids them), the P2W Defense Amplifier (called the best leveling solution, ~2.5M inf/hr at 50, giving 7.5% res-all, 5% def-all, mag 4 mez protection), defense (very hard to combine soft-capped ranged with enough melee def for Venomous Gas's to-hit debuff to close the gap), Rune of Protection (~50% uptime, three powers deep in Sorcery, doubles as a break free), Melee Hybrid (great regen and most mez protection except KB, ~50% uptime, conflicts with Assault hybrid), and Clarion (excludes Barrier/Ageless).
- _Cones_ fight with the in-melee playstyle, so a set like Dark Blast loses much of its appeal; TA/Dark or Storm/Dark are described as having more synergy than Poison/Dark.
- _Healing_ — recommended sources: heal procs in Health and Stamina (Panacea, Power Transfer); Aid Self with Field Medic (counter to the aggressive playstyle); Dark Blast's Life Drain; Water Blast's Dehydrate (water is "one of the best pairings"); Melee Hybrid; Rebirth Destiny; both Defender ATO procs placed in 10-second-recharge powers; and the Entomb absorb proc in Paralytic Poison or Dominate (~200 absorb per cast, "tremendous difference").
- _Power pools_ — skip Concealment, Flight, Medicine, Presence, Teleport. Take Hasten and Leadership (Tactics + Kismet can largely replace accuracy slotting). Other recommendations: Fighting (skipped on the OP's build), Combat Jumping, Force of Will (Mighty Leap, Weaken Resolve as an Achilles trigger, Unleash Potential), Experimentation (Jaunt for fast Venomous Gas insertion, Adrenal Boost), Sorcery (Spirit Ward as a Preventative Medicine mule, Rune of Protection). The OP picked Leadership, Combat Jumping, Hasten and Sorcery.
- _Epics_ — Leviathan: good S/L/cold shield and Hibernate. Scorpion Shield: easy S/L softcap, Focused Accuracy frees slotting, decent pet. Mu: great S/L/energy shield; Power Sink and Conserve Power solve endurance. Soul: meh because Powerboost no longer boosts -special. Dark: Oppressive Gloom synergises with Venomous Gas; perma Soul Drain. Electric: requires the throwaway prerequisite. Power: Conserve Power good, Force of Nature 120s of god-mode resistance with full end crash. Fire: Char alone justifies the pool. Psychic: Dominate is "as good or better than Char," Mass Hypnosis has rare uses, World of Confusion with the Confuse proc is fantastic. The OP ranks Psychic > Dark > Mu.
- _Incarnates_ — Alpha: Musculature Radial (damage, to-hit debuff, defense debuff, recovery) preferred; Cardiac Core solves end and adds res; Nerve helps PPT's accuracy issue. Interface: Reactive Radial for general dot, Degen for big game. Hybrid: Melee Core (resists, regen, mez) or Assault Radial (proc damage). Destiny depends on the rest of the build but Barrier is "incredible" if mez and end are otherwise solved.

**Section 5 — author's build.** The OP picked Poison/Fire because Fire is still "the damage king" and doesn't require heavy proc slotting to perform, freeing up recharge enhancement. Across many respecs the OP settled on a resistance-focused build over soft-capped defense, on the philosophy that resistances and -damage debuffing combine well with Melee Hybrid's regen and Defender ATO absorbs/heals — the OP prefers "predictable and avoidable" failures over the "hero to zero" pattern of squishy defense builds. Mez is covered by cycling Rune of Protection with Melee Hybrid. The OP notes the build would likely be stronger with Cardiac and Barrier instead of Musculature and Ageless, but the latter pair was a conscious DPS choice; they intend to finish out alternate Incarnates for difficult encounters such as the final warwalker standoff in the Tin Mage TF, which they had not yet beaten solo no-insp/temp/Lore. Hybrid is T4 in both Assault Radial and Melee.

### Post 2 — Section: Section 6 — Pylons / TFs / Giant Monsters (Frosticus, 8 April 2020)

**6.1 Pylon.** The OP says they generally wouldn't recommend a Defender for fast pylon clears, but a Poison/Fire can manage it efficiently. Because Poison has no noteworthy -regen, performance is described as "raw" and less proc-dependent than typical high-DPS melee builds. Reported figures: consistent ~1:50 with Hybrid Assault Radial on (~475 DPS) and consistent ~2:15 with Melee Core (off/on doesn't matter, ~410 DPS). A theoretical paper build using Weaken Resolve and other tricks might push ~550 DPS, but the OP labels that gimmicky and not worth building around.

**6.2 Forcing the Task — solo TFs.** The OP describes solo TFs as "one of the best parts of homecoming" and applies a self-imposed rule: no inspirations, no temp powers, never pull out Lore. They acknowledge those tools can complete content, but feel using them blurs what the build itself is doing. The OP notes that this sort of content stresses both DPS and survivability — surviving swarms of ogres and multi-AV encounters takes a well-rounded build. They had only attempted a particular run once on this build, completing it in roughly an hour.

**6.3 Giant Slayer.** The OP frames hunting GMs as "the epitome of heroic feats" and applies the same no-insp/temp/Lore rule. GMs' big challenge is regen, which strongly favors stacking -regen or large amounts of damage. The author argues that some -regen powers in the game are too strong because they fully negate one of the GM/AV mechanics with a single click and leave no room for additional debuffers to contribute.

## Notable replies

The thread's reply discussion is mostly praise and "I'm rolling X/Poison" reports, with the OP returning periodically to answer build questions. A few threads materially extend the guide:

- As @DreadShinobi noted in the 19 January 2021 sub-thread on -res caps, only one instance of each unique -res proc (Achilles' Heel, Fury of the Gladiator, Annihilation) can be active on a target at a time, so doubling up the same proc across two Poison defenders does not stack.
- In the same 19 January 2021 reply, Frosticus confirmed the global -res cap is very high (around -300%) and is rarely reached even in teams, and that Poison doubles up well — pairing two Poison characters opens up the otherwise-skipped ally powers (Antidote, Alkaloid, Elixir of Life).
- In the 13 April 2020 reply to @StridingWind, Frosticus added build advice not in the original sections: in a Poison/Ice build, slot Freeze Ray for procs or a damage set rather than Basilisk's Gaze, and don't under-slot Ice Blast or Bitter Freeze Ray for damage.
- In the 8 June 2020 reply to @Blaque Zeke, Frosticus codified a difficulty preference for the build: solo at +2 while leveling and +3 post-level-shift (which becomes +2 from a debuff perspective), avoiding +3/+4 because the purple patch hits Poison's debuffs disproportionately hard. They reaffirm that for higher difficulties, defense is mandatory just as on any other build, but the guide's philosophy is to lean on -damage + resistance instead.
- A 12 November 2024 update from Frosticus notes two game changes since the original guide: secondary T1s can now be skipped, and Rune of Protection has been nerfed.
