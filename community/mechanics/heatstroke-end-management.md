## Summary

- Endurance is meant to be a real limiter; players commonly blame toggles, but normal attack chains (especially as AoEs and recharge come online) drain end faster than most defensive/offensive toggles do, so attacks need end-reduction slotting.
- More recharge means more attacking, which means more end drain — Hasten and crashing powers (Hasten, Rage, etc.) also dump endurance when they shut off, and that needs to be planned for.
- Big "burst" powers (Lightning Storm, Tornado, pet summons, AoE patches, nukes) cost a huge chunk of the bar per cast; IO-set end reduction can roughly halve their cost (Heatstroke's example: Lightning Storm dropping from ~31.2 to ~16 end per summon).
- Slot Stamina (3 slots, e.g. two boosted End-Mod IOs plus the Performance Shifter +End proc), use Health for Numina/Miracle/Panacea procs, and stack additional Performance Shifter procs in epic-pool autopowers (Superior Conditioning, Physical Perfection) — Willpower Brutes can stack as many as four.
- For builds still struggling at endgame, lean on Cardiac (end reduction on every power), Vigor (T3/T4 adds end reduction and accuracy), or Agility (boosts End Mod and recharge), and grab the Portal Jockey / Born in Battle / Atlas Medallion / Marshal accolades for free Health and Endurance bonuses.

## Verbatim excerpt

The guide is a single OP post (last edited 2025-06-13). Subsequent posts by Heatstroke in the thread are non-substantive bumps ("bumpity", "bump", "Bumpity") and are not concatenated. The numbered structure below preserves Heatstroke's original 11-point outline; long passages are paraphrased to respect copyright, with brief in-quotes excerpts where the wording is distinctive.

**1) Endurance is a designed limiting factor — and toggles are usually not the real culprit.**
Heatstroke opens by stressing that end management cannot be ignored or "bargained with"; it has to be planned around. He pushes back on the common complaint that "my toggles are killing my end," arguing that attacks drain more endurance than toggles do, especially as characters level into stronger and AoE attacks. He uses a worked Fire/Fire Tank example: a standard defense toggle costs about 0.26 end/sec, an offensive toggle averages 0.52 end/sec, and the basic Fire Melee attack Scorch costs 4.37 end per cast (1s activation, 3s recharge). Over a 9-second window, three Scorch casts drain 13.11 end versus 9.36 end for Fire Shield + Plasma Shield + Blazing Aura combined. He notes toggles do hinder recovery somewhat because they tick constantly, and flags exceptionally heavy toggles like Choking Cloud (0.65/s) and Focused Accuracy (0.78/s). He also points out that some sets are extremely toggle-heavy (Dark Armor running ten toggles is his example) and can still be managed.

**2) More recharge means faster end drain.**
Hasten and IO-set recharge bonuses make you attack more often, which drains end more often. He also notes that when Hasten shuts off it costs about 15 endurance.

**3) Watch power crashes and recovery debuffs.**
Beyond full crashes, several powers drain endurance or hinder recovery on shutdown. Hasten and Rage (Super Strength) are called out as examples that need to be accounted for in the build.

**4) Big powers drain big endurance.**
Summons and patch powers are the worst offenders. Heatstroke's Storm Summoning examples: Lightning Storm costs 31.2 end to activate ("almost 1/3 of your entire end bar"), Tornado costs 20, controller pet summons run roughly 16–20 end, and stacking patches like Freezing Rain plus Ice Storm can also burn through a third of the bar. AoE attacks and nukes cost more by design, which is why slotting end reduction (SO or IO) into attacks matters. He also warns that fully proc'd-out attacks typically have **zero** end reduction, which can wreck the bar.

**5) Learn to build your own characters.**
He encourages using Mids Reborn and learning to read the numbers, and pushes back on endgame-only builds that "consistently sacrifice endurance management" to chase defense or recharge and then paper over the gap with a T4 Ageless — which falls apart while leveling or when exemped. IO sets are the lever to bake end reduction into individual powers; in his Lightning Storm example, slotting drops the cost from 31.2 to 16 end per summon, letting him "summon TWO clouds for the price of one."

**6) Slot Stamina.**
His recommendation: three slots — two End-Mod IOs (consider boosting them to +5) plus the Performance Shifter +Endurance proc. Going beyond three slots is only worth it for set bonuses. The proc adds about 10 end on each fire and "fires often."

**7) Use Health to boost recovery.**
Slot Health with Numina's +Regen/Recovery, Miracle +Recovery, and Panacea +Hit Points/Endurance. The Panacea proc also adds about 10 end on fire. He notes these can be expensive, but reward merits and converters (rolling other Numina/Miracle pieces) are workarounds.

**8) Slot procs that help endurance into other powers.**
The Theft of Essence +Endurance proc in targeted heals (heals that need accuracy) is highlighted as an excellent option.

**9) Use the melee epic pools to stack Performance Shifter procs.**
Tankers/Brutes/Scrappers/Stalkers can use Energy Mastery or Body Mastery powers like Superior Conditioning and Physical Perfection as extra homes for Performance Shifter +End (which is not unique and stacks). Procs work best in **auto** powers, since they fire more reliably than in clicks/toggles. Per Heatstroke's stacking notes: Tankers with Energy Mastery and Scrappers with Body Mastery can stack 2; Willpower Tanks/Scrappers can stack 3; Brutes (Energy Mastery) and Stalkers (Body Mastery) can stack 3; **Willpower Brutes can stack 4.**

**10) Use the right Incarnate Alpha for your problem.**
He emphasizes the difference between end **Reduction** (cuts the cost of using powers) and end **Modification** (boosts how much you recover). Cardiac reduces every power's end cost and is his go-to for high-recharge, high-AoE builds; he says some builds need Cardiac even after all the other slotting. Agility boosts End Modification (lifting Stamina, Physical Perfection, and any drain powers) and also boosts recharge — a double-edged benefit. Vigor, often overlooked because its primary function is healing, picks up end reduction at T3/T4 and an accuracy bonus, which he found useful on a Regen/Psi Sentinel.

**11) Pursue Accolades for free Health and Endurance.**
Credit given to Hyperstrike. Portal Jockey / Born in Battle grants +5% Health and +5% Endurance; Atlas Medallion / Marshal grants an additional +5% Endurance.

## Notable replies

- As **@Hyperstrike** noted in his 2024-01-31 reply (which Heatstroke explicitly cites in point 11), the accolade bonuses sound minor but stack to "completely change the performance profile" of a character, and the Performance Shifter +End proc in Stamina is roughly equivalent to an L50 End-Mod IO and, after the first two slots, becomes better than an End-Mod IO because of diminishing returns. This reply is the source of the Accolades point folded into the OP's section 11.
