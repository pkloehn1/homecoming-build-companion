## Summary

- "Early Bloomer" leveling: slot attuned IOs and proc-heavy set pieces from the moment they unlock, raise difficulty early, and use unslotters/respecs aggressively. The guide is a level-by-level shopping list of which procs and sets are worth the inf at each unlock tier (1, 7, 10, 12, 17/18, 22, 25, 27, 32, 47, 50).
- Highest-leverage early procs (mostly slottable at level 7 attuned): Panacea +HP/End in Health, Miracle +Recovery in Health, Kismet +ToHit in a defense power, Steadfast Protection +Def in a resist power, Achilles' Heel and Fury of the Gladiator -Res, Karma / Steadfast / Blessing of the Zephyr KB protection. The "indispensable starter pack" Yomo always buys is Panacea, Kismet, Shield Wall, Steadfast, Performance Shifter, Reactive Defenses, plus both ATO sets.
- Damage procs scale by _character_ level, not proc level; effect procs (-Res, etc.) do not scale. Most procs behave the same attuned or not — Yomo prefers attuned for aesthetics. Auto-power procs (e.g. Numina in Health) effectively maintain a near-permanent buff because the 2-minute buff is reapplied long before it expires.
- Bug list to exploit: most level-21 attuned sets unlock at 17 (Endurance Modification sets are the fixed exception); some "level 21" attuned sets are even more bugged and slot as low as level 7 (Ukase confirms attuned Performance Shifter EndMod IOs slot at 7, but the +End proc does not). Conversely, dynafireca clarifies that the Mids/in-game _display_ of attuned IOs above their cap is a display bug — actual enhancement values still cap at the listed level cap.
- Endgame switch (level 47–50): replace non-proc attuned PvP IOs with level-50 boosted +5 PvP IOs to lock in stronger numerical values that ride exemplar all the way down to level 7. At 50, catalyze ATOs and Winter-Os to Superior immediately for stronger set bonuses and proc effects (catalysts drop on 50s with an 18-hour lockout, so stockpile them in a base bin).

### Post 1 — Section: OP / "Early Blooming" leveling guide (2020-10-16, last edited 2024-09-24)

[In memoriam for @Robotech_Master]

A lot of people like to rush their characters to level 50 and beyond before slotting their meticulously crafted Mid's build. Some of them don't even slot an enhancement until they hit 50! This guide is probably not for those people.

I've been playing a with a leveling strategy that I'm terming "Early Blooming." With this strategy, I'm slotting attuned IOs and IO sets as soon as possible for maximum effectiveness and raising difficulty settings early and often. As I level, I use unslotters and respecs with reckless abandon, and the results are pretty impressive. The purpose of this guide is not to tell you how to do this, but rather to reinforce what IOs are most useful at surprisingly early levels.

[EDIT note from Yomo: he has come around on stun procs over time — "There are some interesting oddball uses for some but that's probably another guide." Focus is on procs of all shapes and sizes plus set bonuses (endurance, accuracy, recharge, defense) that boost sub-30 performance dramatically.]

Note: I assume that all set IOs are purchased attuned, including procs. I realize that attuned procs are not generally superior to non-attuned procs. However, I find them more aesthetically appealing. Every set IO is bucketed in the AH as both a leveled IO and as an attuned IO, so you can buy either for the same price. It is also possible, even easy, to sell a leveled IO on one character and buy the attuned version on another for the same listing.

A quick note on procs: damage procs scale by level of the character, not by level of the proc. Effect procs (like -Res) do not scale. Most, but not all, procs will behave the same whether or not they are attuned. Personally, I use attuned, but again, that's personal preference. EDIT: see level 47 for some notable exceptions.

You can find all this information either in-game or at https://hcwiki.cityofheroes.dev/wiki/Enhancement_Sets

**Level 1 – P2W enhancements**

The P2W vendor offers five different free enhancements; all are D/R/proc, you can slot them starting at level 1, and they are effective through level 20. Each is unique, so although you can get multiple copies of each one, you can only slot one and you cannot sell them to vendors. Plus, there is now an easy upgrade button where you can upgrade all your slotted SOs to +3 every level for a bunch of inf. I usually upgrade every level. Just don't let it drop below -3.

On a pure aspect-enhancement basis you'll get more bang for the buck slotting SOs until invention origins get high enough in level. If it's a drag to remember to upgrade every level, put in attuned invention IOs as soon as you can. If you are looking for pure aspect enhancement, switching over to invention sets around 30–35 makes sense. But if the set bonuses you are seeking are important (like +recharge) then earlier is better than later. I usually start making the shift somewhere between 17 and 32. A good shortcut: when will I get better accuracy from using a set instead of using a +3 SO?

**Level 7 – Level 10 sets**

[Yomo notes that every "level 10" set is slottable at level 7, except the truly attuned-only sets: ATOs, Winter-Os, Universal Damage.]

Critical procs you can slot at 7:

- **Panacea +Hit Points/Endurance (Healing) unique** — goes in Health. You may want to add more procs to Health later, but this one will prove useful your entire career. ~10mm.
- **Kismet +to hit (Accuracy) (Defense) unique** — critical for early game play. Without accuracy bonuses early it's extremely frustrating to keep missing only to have the streak breaker activate on Brawl. If you don't have an early power that slots Defense, consider picking Combat Jumping or Hover. 3–4mm. EDIT: keep it for the long haul if you don't have to-hit-debuff resistance — a pack of Agony Mages will ruin your day otherwise.
- **Steadfast Protection Resistance/Defense (Resist Damage) unique** — if you have a resist damage power, slot this somewhere. Tough is the fallback if you don't have a resist set yet.
- **Achilles' Heel Chance for Resistance Debuff (Defense Debuff)** — bias confessed: Yomo will slot -Res procs until the cows come home. Effect does not stack; multiple copies / external triggers only refresh duration. 3–5mm.
- **Fury of the Gladiator Chance for -Res (PBAoE) unique** — the cows have not come home yet. 9–10mm.
- **Karma KB Protection (Defense) / Steadfast Protection KB Protection (Resist Damage) / Blessing of the Zephyr KB Protection (Universal Travel)** — each gives 4 points of KB protection. Even on characters that will eventually have -KB powers, these are worth slotting temporarily at low levels. 2–4mm each.
- **Call To Arms +Pet Defense (Recharge Intensive Pets) unique** — defense bonus to a Mastermind's pets in radius. One of six MM proc must-haves.

Useful procs (still at 7):

- **Theft of Essence +Endurance (Accurate Healing) unique** — blue-bar refill. _(Yomo notes elsewhere he uses this more than he used to: "I hate missing, running out of endurance, and having my recharge debuffed. Generally I will make sure I have this in a travel power.")_
- **Energy Manipulator % for Stun (8s mag 2) (Endurance Modification)** — must-slot in any AoE endurance power that affects mobs; proc rate will be at or near max in autohit powers and can effectively auto-stun minions.

Damage procs at 7 (scale with character level):

- Gladiator's Strike (Melee) Smashing — 8–10mm
- Eradication (PBAoE) Energy — 3–5mm
- Gladiator's Javelin (Ranged) Toxic — 8–10mm
- Javelin Volley (Targeted AoE) Lethal — 8–10mm
- Cacophony (Confuse) Energy — 1mm
- Neuronic Shutdown (Hold) Psionic — 3–5mm
- Gladiator's Net (Hold) Lethal — 8–10mm
- Explosive Strike (Knockback) Smashing — 1mm

Useful sets at 7:

- **Call to Arms (Recharge Intensive Pets)** — recharge bonus at 4 slots, S/L resist at 5; or frankenslot early and swap to attuned Expedient Reinforcements later. 2–4mm/piece.
- **Regenerative Tissue (Healing)** — recharge bonus at 5 slots; cheap interim solution before Panacea/Preventative Medicine.
- **Panacea (Healing)** — gold standard for a 5-slot heal, but expensive. ~10mm+.
- **Kismet (Defense)** — small recharge bonus at 5; holding set for future Red Fortune / Reactive Defenses / LotG. 2–4mm/piece.
- **Impervious Skin (Resist Damage)** — 5 slots for recharge bonus; only recharge bonus you'll get out of a resist set. Cheap, <1mm/piece.
- **Shield Wall (Defense) / Gladiator's Armor (Resist Damage)** — expensive holding sets but decent bonuses; 8–10mm/piece (typical PvP set pricing).

**Level 10 – Attuned Sets (ATOs, Winter-Os, Universal Damage)**

When they say level-10 sets they mean level-10 — no +3 nonsense. They scale, so you never need to move them.

- **ATOs** — generally worth slotting at some point; some are must-haves (Stalker procs), some are meh (Dominators, with mez modification rather than damage). Always check the 2- and 3-slot bonuses as well as the procs — that's where the real cost-benefit lives.
- **Winter-Os** — for a fire farmer, mandatory. Otherwise Yomo finds better/cheaper solutions and never uses the procs. Avalanche or Blistering Cold for melee characters because of the melee-defense bonus. He has never slotted an Entomb. ~20–23mm a pop. EDIT: increasingly likes the slow/recharge-debuff resistance that comes as early as the 2-piece bonus.
- **Overwhelming Force** — set bonuses are OK; what makes the set is twofold: slottable in any damaging power, and the proc has a flat 20% chance (no PPM) to cause knockdown on activation, working up to bosses. EDIT: per CoD it's actually a 25% chance to KD any non-AV-class enemy, and the radial pulse is 100% to hit any non-pet within a 4-foot radius. 10mm+.

**Level 12 – Adding Stealth**

Travel sets are basically a stealth delivery system (much like escargot is a garlic-butter delivery system).

Useful procs:

- **Edict of the Master +Defense Bonus for Pets (Pet Damage) unique** — another MM must-have. 1–2mm.
- **Impervium Armor +Psionic Resistance (Resist Damage)** — plug some psi holes. NOT unique — slot up to five of them across powers to stack psi resistance. 3–4mm.
- **Celerity +Stealth (Running) / Unbounded Leap (Leaping) / Freebird (Flight) / TimeSpace Manipulation (Teleport) +Stealth uniques** — Celerity in Sprint is essentially a must-have; the others are optional depending on travel preference. 1–2mm each.

Useful sets:

- **Impervium Armor** — not the greatest set, but useful for psi-resistance frankenslotting.

**Level 17/18 – The Turning Point**

Until they fix the bug, every set that's _supposed_ to start at level 21 (in attuned form) is available at 17 — except the new Endurance Modification sets. Some "level 21" attuned sets are _really_ bugged and slot as low as level 7; Yomo flags those with an asterisk but doesn't list them because of limited usefulness. (See @Ukase below for the Performance Shifter EndMod-at-7 confirmation.)

Very useful procs:

- **Miracle +Recovery (Healing) unique** — if you don't already have a Panacea proc in Health, add Miracle at 17. 4–5mm. Note: AH price display is often bugged — if it looks like 100k, double-check before listing.
- **Performance Shifter +Endurance (Endurance Modification) unique** — slot it in Stamina, with a Kismet Accuracy as well. 3–5mm.
- **Gaussian's Chance for Buildup\* (To Hit Buff) unique** — not in every build, but in a lot of them. 2–3mm.
- **Force Feedback Chance for Recharge\* (Knockback)** — slot it early and often as an ersatz Hasten replacement. When taking the Fighting pool, take Kick and 1-slot it with FF — random kicks giving +100% recharge for 5s. Not unique. 2–3mm.
- **Annihilation Chance for -Res (Targeted AoE)** — Yomo loves -Res. Particularly nice in an epic-pool TAoE for melee characters where the long base recharge guarantees procs across most targets. 2–3mm.

Useful procs:

- **Preventive Medicine +Absorb (Healing) unique** — works in passive-trigger fashion; you don't need to use the power for the proc to fire.
- Lockdown's +Hold proc (Hold), Hecatomb's Damage proc once you go purple, etc. Damage procs available at 17: Apocalypse-tier substitutes like Touched by the Beyond, Will of the Controller / Stalker effect-procs, Coercive Persuasion's confuse, Trap of the Hunter's lethal proc, Perfect Zinger's psi-damage taunt proc, Touch of Lady Gray's neg-energy proc — 1–2mm each.

Useful sets:

- **Kinetic Combat (Melee)** — Oh yeah! 4-slot it (skip the KB proc) for the S/L and melee defense, plus a touch of F/C resistance and some health. Sometimes Yomo rides this set all the way to 50, but generally adds a slot or two to frankenslot. 2–4mm/piece.
- **Multi Strike (PBAoE) / Scirocco's Dervish (PBAoE)** — generally Yomo uses ATOs for PBAoEs instead, but these aren't bad and they ride to 50.
- **Entropic Chaos (Ranged)** — 5-slot recharge bonus is great; procs with occasional heals are nice icing. (Decimation eventually replaces it later.)
- **Preventive Medicine (Healing)** — 8.75% recharge bonus plus resistance and end/health. Good to go from 17 onward, adding slots as available. 2–4mm/piece.
- **Reactive Defenses (Defense)** — almost identical bonuses to Preventive Medicine, but the 6-slot recharge gives up the ability to also slot a LotG +recharge. 2–4mm/piece.
- **Unbreakable Guard (Resist Damage)** — generally Yomo's favorite Res set: 4 slots gets melee defense, 2 gets endurance discount. 2–4mm/piece.
- **Malaise's Illusions (Confuse)** — 5-slot it early (6 if available) and you won't touch it again until purples.
- **Glimpse of the Abyss (Fear)** — Yomo prefers Cloud Senses if the power takes ToHit Debuff sets, but Glimpse gets +Health, accuracy, and a couple of other useful bonuses.
- **Perfect Zinger (Taunt)** — lesser recharge bonus at 4 slots, but the proc is always nice. 1–2mm/piece.
- **Adjusted Targeting (To Hit Buff)** — 5-slot for 5% recharge plus accuracy; 6-slot adds S/L res to the pos/neg energy res. 1–2mm/piece.
- **Gaussian's (To Hit Buff)** — generally just slot the proc, or 6-slot for defense across the board.
- **Dampened Spirits / Dark Watcher's Despair (To Hit Debuff)** — included only for the recharge bonuses; Yomo can't recall ever personally slotting them.

**Level 18 – New Endurance Modification Sets (and Sudden Acceleration)**

Useful procs:

- **Power Transfer Chance for Self-Heal (Endurance Modification)** — no personal experience, but mentioned in cross-references. (See the +HP/+Regen Proc Cheat Sheet thread for full mechanics.)
- **Performance Shifter Chance for +End / Synapse's Shock (Endurance Modification)** — 4-slot and 5-slot end/recharge bonuses respectively. Could also include Preemptive Optimization, but Yomo generally goes Synapse's. 1–5mm/piece.
- **Sudden Acceleration (Knockback)** — if you want knockdown-iness, set bonuses are good to great all the way to 6 (recharge), but no damage in the set.
- **Synapse's Shock (Endurance Modification)** — 3 slots gets slow resistance (Yomo has come to appreciate that); 5 slots adds +6.25% recharge.

**Level 22 – LotG and Friends**

- **Luck of the Gambler 7.5% Recharge (Defense)** — slot up to five separate LotG +recharge IOs. The Rule of Five is separate from these and from other 7.5% recharge bonuses. Will probably be slotted in every character. 5–7mm.

Useful procs:

- **Sovereign Right +Resistance (Pet Damage)** — another MM special. 1–3mm.
- **Aegis +Psionic / +Mez resistance (Resist Damage) uniques** — useful psi/mez plugs.

Useful sets (intermediate goalposts):

- **Touch of Death (Melee)** — 6-slot for the melee defense bonus, plus resist and a neg-energy proc; capped at 40. 2–4mm/piece.
- **Decimation (Ranged Damage)** — 5-slot 6.25% recharge with better stats than Entropic Chaos. Skip the proc. 2–4mm/piece.
- **Red Fortune (Defense)** — better survival than LotG when you can give up some of the recharge: includes 5% recharge at 5 slots and ranged defense at 6. 3–4mm/piece.
- **Aegis (Resist Damage)** — formerly fire-farmer staple, now cheap. 1–3mm/piece.

**Level 27 – The Home Stretch**

Useful procs:

- **Numina's Convalescence +Regen/+Recovery (Healing) unique** — Yomo doesn't always slot it but uses it sometimes.

Damage procs:

- Mako's Bite (Melee) Lethal — 1–2mm
- Obliteration (PBAoE) Smashing — 2–5mm
- Bombardment (Targeted AoE) Fire — 1–3mm
- Touch of the Nictus (Accurate Healing) Negative Energy — 1–3mm

Useful sets:

- **Crushing Impact (Melee) / Thunderstrike (Ranged Damage)** — budget pieces, useful but not stellar; <1mm/piece.
- **Mako's Bite (Melee)** — resistance is fine, but 6-slot ranged defense is the icing. 1–3mm/piece.
- **Obliteration (PBAoE)** — more melee defense; recharge and melee defense at 5 and 6 slots respectively, plus a nice 2-slot resist bonus. Stats aren't ideal for damage auras (low end-reduction). 2–5mm/piece.
- **Bombardment (Targeted AoE)** — fire damage proc, decent set bonuses. 1–3mm/piece.

[Yomo's strategy note on level-30 sets: keep the lower-tier attuned set rather than upgrading, because most lower-tier sets (e.g. Cloud Senses) have superior bonuses to their level-30+ siblings (Siphon Insight). At endgame he sometimes swaps out attuned Cloud Senses for level-30 versions boosted to +5 — but only because he's "rich, RICH!"]

**Level 32 – Sting, Stang, Stung**

Only thing coming online at 35 is **Sting of the Manticore** — good snipe set with good bonuses and a toxic damage proc. 1–3mm/piece.

**Level 47 – Hammy and Non-Attuned PvP IOs**

There are some Hami-Os available before 50, but Yomo doesn't think about them until 47.

Important upgrade: replace your non-proc attuned PvP IOs (e.g. attuned A/D and A/D/E from a Gladiator's set) with **level-50 boosted +5 PvP IOs** at 47. The attuned versions act as level 47 only; the +5'd 50s act as level 55 IOs at 47, and continue to act as +5 even when you exemplar all the way down to level 7. There's no point in replacing the proc itself.

**Level 50 – Endgame**

Huge step up that has nothing to do with incarnates:

- **Catalyze ATOs at level 50** to convert them to Superior. Two effects: better set bonuses, and stronger procs (increased effect or increased PPM). Do this at 50 ASAP.
- **Catalyze Winter-Os at 50** to upgrade them to Superior with similar bonus and proc improvements.

Let me know if I got anything wrong and I'll be happy to amend this guide.

### Post 8 — Section: Yomo's indispensable starter pack (2023-12-07)

"Yomo," people ask me, "what enhancements do you personally find indispensable?"\*

Any new character will buy the following, all attuned (because I like the look, although it does make a difference for some enhancements), in this order:

- **Panacea proc** — Health, as soon as level 7
- **Kismet proc** — defense power (probably Combat Jumping), as soon as level 7
- **Shield Wall proc** — defense power (probably Combat Jumping), as soon as level 7
- **Steadfast Protection proc** — resist-damage power (probably Tough), as soon as level 7 — but if Tough is the slot, that's level 14
- **Performance Shifter proc** — Stamina, level 17
- **Reactive Defenses proc** — defense power (probably Combat Jumping), as soon as level 17
- Usually both sets of ATOs. Don't always use them depending on AT, generally start to slot them somewhere in the 20s.

It is difficult for me to imagine ever building a character that didn't have all of these enhancements.

\* In fact, no one has ever asked me this. Consider it poetic license.
