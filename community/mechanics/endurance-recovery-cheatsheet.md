## Summary

- Vanden converts every common +Recovery source into a single shared unit — **End/Min** (since Base Recovery = 100/min and chance-for-+Endurance procs operate on procs-per-minute) — so SOs, level-50 IOs, level-50+5 boosted IOs, Alpha tier-4 boosts, and unique procs can all be compared on the same axis. The four uniques are: Numina 10, Miracle 15, Performance Shifter proc 15, Panacea proc 22.5 End/Min (later disputed; see Notable replies).
- Per-power slotting tables (End/Min) are given for: Stamina/Inexhaustible (base 25), Quick Recovery / Accelerate Metabolism / Light Form (base 30), Blaster Sustains (minus Drain Psyche, base 50), Physical Perfection (base 12.5), and Arachnos Soldier/Widow Stamina (base 26.25 atop a 105 base recovery). Each table shows first/second/third End-Mod SO, first/second/third lvl-50 End Mod IO, first/second lvl-50+5 boosted End Mod IO, Agility Core Paragon, and Agility/Musculature Radial Paragon contributions, all already discounted for ED.
- Practical takeaways: a second lvl-50 End Mod IO in Stamina (+10.23 End/Min) gives less than what Physical Perfection's base provides (12.5), so once you have Physical Perfection you can swap a Stamina End Mod IO for a proc; the third End Mod IO/SO in any power is heavily ED-cropped; +50+5 boosted IOs measurably outperform plain lvl-50 IOs because the +Recovery portion is on Schedule A. The charts assume you don't mix SOs and IOs (mixing doesn't bypass ED).
- The Ageless destiny gives 100 End/Min even at the lowest buff level — effectively "you will not run out of endurance, period" for most builds — and stacks an additional 33–45 End/Min from Agility Core / Musculature Radial alphas. Recovery set bonuses are also denominated in this unit: a 2.5% recovery set bonus = 2.5 End/Min (it scales slightly higher for VEATs but not enough to matter).
- Increasing max endurance increases your recovery proportionally (Bopper, page 2): at the 100% base recovery rate, going from 100 to 105 max end means recovering 5 extra End/Min; at 200% recovery, that becomes 210 End/Min instead of 200. So +Max End accolades and set bonuses act as a flat multiplier on every End/Min number in the cheat sheet.

### Post 1 — Section: Charts (universal, Stamina/Inexhaustible, QR/AM/LightForm, Blaster Sustains, Physical Perfection, VEAT)

The Recovery values are given in **Endurance per Minute**, rather than the typical in-game Endurance per Second, because Endurance per Minute results in nice, easy-to-follow numbers; Base Recovery is 100 endurance in one minute, and the chance for +Endurance procs operate on procs per minute, so working in End/Min means everything's using the same units and easy to compare.

### Post 2 — Section: Inexhaustible / Gamma Boost note (2019-08-23)

Inexhaustible has the same recovery boost as Stamina, so I just added it to the Stamina chart. It gets another 10% buff with Efficient Adaptation, but that buff ignores enhancements, so you don't have to worry about it when planning your slots. The charts give numbers in a straightforward "you will get this much endurance with this enhancement slotting" way.

I'm not sure if Gamma Boost would really fit in this thread. I could make a chart for it when buffing Recovery at its maximum value, but since it can vary so much in gameplay that might not be meaningful information. It's also, like you say, very fuzzy. I checked it in-game and even at 100% health, when the Recovery boost should be at its maximum, it's less than the real numbers in the info window say. _Edited August 28, 2019._

### Post 5 — Section: Mixing SOs and IOs (2019-08-31)

The charts assume you're not mixing SOs and IOs. You can't bypass ED by doing that, so if you do, ED will just reduce the enhancements' effectiveness, hard, once you start going over 95% enhancement, same as always.

### Post 6 — Section: Why Lifegiving Spores isn't in the chart (2019-09-08)

Lifegiving Spores isn't a good fit for this cheat sheet, since you can't really perma it; it only affects a small area and you have to toggle it off to move it, and wait for it to recharge, so it can't be a consistent, reliable source of endurance.

### Post 8 — Section: Ageless destiny (2019-09-20)

For the Ageless destinies that give +Recovery, it's 100 end per minute even at the lowest level of the buff. That's pretty much "you will not run out of endurance, period" levels of Recovery for many characters. You can get another 33 or 45 end per minute by also equipping the right Agility or Musculature alphas.

### Post 9 — Section: Theft of Essence in Frigid Protection (2019-10-15)

Well, I'm not the proc math guy, so I can't give you solid numbers, but I tested Theft of Essence in Frigid Protection. It can go off with no target, but the proc rate is very low from what I can see. It has additional chances to go off for every enemy in the AoE at proc time, which will make it slightly better in actual gameplay than while hanging out with Ms. Liberty, but since I don't know the proc math for AoEs offhand that's about as specific as I can be.

### Post 10 — Section: Worked example — leveling Brute (2022-08-01)

As an example of how these charts can be useful, I'm currently leveling a Brute, and in the early 40s I realized that my endurance issues were basically solved; this character is simply not running out of endurance. My build calls for Physical Perfection, however, and a quick consultation of the chart showed that Physical Protection gives more +Recovery than a second level 50 EndMod IO in Stamina. That means that when I pick up Physical Perfection, I can replace an EndMod IO in Stamina with a Power Transfer proc, and put another Power Transfer proc in Physical Protection instead of the originally planned Performance Shifter proc. A nice boost to survivability.

- **@Teirusu (page 1, 2019-08-31):** Materially corrects the Panacea-proc value in the OP. As @Teirusu noted: "I'm pretty certain that your calculated number for the Panacea Proc is showing it as nearly twice the effectiveness then it actually is. The Panacea is a PPM 3 while the Performance Shifter is a PPM 1.5, but while the Shifter is a 100% chance of 10 percent of your END bar, the Panacea is a 50/50% chance of either HP or END, which is only 7.5% of your END bar. So you actually get ticks of END at about the same rate as the Shifter proc, but less total END. So it's actually 11.25 End/Min, slightly better than the Numina." Vanden's Post 4 confirms the 3 PPM measurement but does not update the chart's 22.5 End/Min figure; readers should treat the Panacea proc as ~11.25 End/Min for endurance accounting if Teirusu's math holds.
- **@SmalltalkJava (page 1, 2019-09-03):** Surfaces Lifegiving Spores (Nature Affinity ground-target toggle) as a missing +Recovery power, which prompts Vanden's Post 6/7 explanation of why it isn't chartable (can't perma; toggle interrupts on movement; uncertain tick rate).
- **@Frostweaver (page 1, 2019-10-15):** Surfaces the Theft of Essence proc question — Mids shows it as 58% chance for End vs Performance Shifter's 25%, both with the same 10% boost; does that net out to more End in Frigid Protection? Vanden's Post 9 tests it and reports the proc rate is very low without a target, slightly better in saturated AoE conditions, but he can't quantify AoE proc math.
- **@dtj714 (page 2, 2022-08-11):** Surfaces Light Form as missing — Vanden adds it to the QR/AM/Light Form (30% Recovery boost) row in Post 11.
- **@Bopper (page 2, 2023-12-02):** Materially extends the guide by codifying the relationship between **Max End and recovery rate**. As @Bopper noted in reply (page 2): "You start the game off with a base recovery of 100%, which translates to 'you will recover all of your endurance in one minute of gameplay.' So if your max endurance goes up from 100 to 105, then you will recover an additional 5 endurance over that minute. If you have 200% recovery (+100% from base), then you will recover all of your endurance in 60s / 200% = 30s. That translates to getting 200 endurance per minute if your max is 100, or getting 210 endurance per minute if your max is 105." This confirms that every End/Min figure in the OP charts scales linearly with Max End buffs (accolades, +HP/+End set bonuses), and that 200% recovery → 30-second full refill.
- **@Zombra (page 2, 2025-03-10):** Codifies a build implication — once you understand End/Min framing, the Superior Conditioning power (Body Mastery / equivalent epic) becomes "much better than it appears at a glance" because its base contribution lands at the same scale as Stamina-class powers in the chart.
