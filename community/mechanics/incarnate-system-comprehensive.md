# A Comprehensive Guide to the Incarnate System

## Summary

- The Incarnate window has three tabs (Equip, Create, Convert); each slot's tree requires building two Tier 3 powers to combine into a Tier 4, and Common/Uncommon/Rare/Very Rare components feed each tier.
- Build the Alpha slot from Shards (drops, WST Notices of the Well, Mender Ramiel arc, level-50 TFs/SFs) since Shards have few other uses, but switch to Threads for the Tier 4 Alpha to avoid waiting weeks to accumulate two Notices for a Favor of the Well.
- After Alpha, prioritize Lore and Destiny next because their Tier 3 versions grant additional Incarnate Level Shifts (effectively level 53) that apply to Trials, Dark Astoria, and similar Incarnate content (oddly not Apex/Tin Mage), making you on par with level 54 mobs.
- Save Empyrean Merits — every Rare crafted from Empyreans costs 8, every Very Rare costs 30, and after Vet 11 you stop getting Thread floods from leveling; converting 50 Empyreans into a Transcendent Merit is the efficient way to forward Incarnate progress to alts.
- Incandescence Destiny is recommended for nearly every character even if another Destiny is preferred — its on-demand league-wide Assemble-the-Team (every ~2 minutes) plus knockback/stun makes it broadly useful for travel, league rallying, and offensive openers; the Tier 1 60-Thread version is enough to gain the core utility.

## Verbatim excerpt

The Incarnate system, explained in-game as a way to become a god-like being yourself, is the post-level-50 endgame power and content system that NCSoft implemented in the final days of the live version of City of Heroes. As far as why you "want" them, you're going to be given them whether you want them or not. They add a little extra zest to playing level 50s through 45+ content, and being effectively level 50+1 in regular content is a great reward for the effort you put into getting all the way to 50.

The Incarnate system is confusing partly because it was introduced very late in the live game's life cycle and never got the streamlining other systems received, and partly because SCORE had to patch over the old VIP-paywalled design rather than redesign it from scratch. That's why we still have two largely incompatible Incarnate crafting systems (Shards vs. Threads) and two sets of Incarnate merits that can't be used for very much.

UNDERSTANDING THE INCARNATE POWERS WINDOW

A useful macro: `/macro INC "windowtoggle Incarnate"`. The window has three tabs: Equip, Create, and Convert.

EQUIP shows all your Incarnate power slots, what's unlocked, and lets you slot a created power. There's a 5-minute cooldown on slotting new powers (zone changes can reset it; you can craft and slot immediately after exiting completed Incarnate trials in some cases).

CREATE is one of the least intuitive UIs in the game. The middle area is a tree with one orb at the bottom, two on row two, four on row three, and two on row four. Each recipe uses two Common components plus one component of escalating rarity as you climb. Each tier above the first uses the power below it as a component. The Tier 4 ability uses any two Tier 3 abilities — there are six Tier 4 recipes covering each pairing.

CONVERT lets you create components out of Shards, Threads, or Empyrean Merits, and "sidegrade" components to other components.

INCARNATE SHARD: Common components cost 4 Shards; Uncommon cost a Common + 8 Shards (12 total). Notice of the Well = 4 Uncommon + 40 Shards (88 Shards) + 12.5M Inf. Favor of the Well = 2 Notices + 32 Shards. Notices are given out one per week as Weekly Strike Target rewards, so don't pay 88 shards + 12.5M Inf for one. "Upgrade" lets you convert 10 Shards → 10 Threads once every 20 hours (or 10 Shards → 5 Threads at any time), Notice → 40 Threads, Favor → 100 Threads.

INCARNATE THREAD: Common = 20 Threads, Uncommon = 60, Rare = 4 Uncommon + 100 Threads + 25M Inf (340 Threads total), Very Rare = 4 Rare + 100M Inf (1360 Threads total). The "Incarnate XP" option converts 1 Thread → 50,000 Incarnate XP, which used to matter on Live but now isn't worth it because Homecoming awards Incarnate XP from regular play. From Vet Levels 1–11 you'll get 8 installments of 120 Threads on level-up.

ASTRAL/EMPYREAN MERITS: 1 Astral = 4 Threads. 1 Empyrean = 20 Threads, or 8 Empyreans = a Rare component, or 30 Empyreans = a Very Rare. Empyreans are awarded on level-up every 3 Vet levels (20 each at Vet 3–24, 15 at 27–48, 10 at 51–69, 5 at 72–99), plus 4 per successful Hamidon Raid. Don't convert Empyreans to Reward Merits — Reward Merits are easy and the conversion can't be reversed. Use Luna on Ouroboros to swap Astrals/Empyreans, or 50 Empyreans → 1 Transcendent Merit for emailing to alts.

COMMON/UNCOMMON/RARE/VERY RARE: Sidegrade swaps a component for another of the same rarity (costs Threads); Downgrade converts to one rarity lower (no Thread refund); Breakdown returns a fraction of the component's Thread cost.

THE INCARNATE PROCESS: You're not locked into the first Incarnate power you build. By Vet Level 99 you'll have enough drops to build several powers per slot. A Tanker/Brute might keep both Resilient and Spiritual Alphas at Tier 4 for swapping toughness vs. recharge.

ALPHA SLOT: Always-on, functions as an extra Enhancement on every power that can slot that category, and partly bypasses Enhancement Diversification caps. Powers that don't take Enhancements (temp powers, prestige powers, other Incarnates) are unaffected. Tier 1 enhances one attribute; Tier 4 enhances three attributes well or six moderately. Tier 3 and Tier 4 grant a Level Shift that applies to all content when exemplared to level 45 or higher. Pick the Alpha that boosts the most things you do use and the fewest things you don't.

JUDGEMENT: A nuke. Every couple of minutes, high damage, high target count. Considerations: damage type (Smashing/Lethal in Mighty/Vorpal vs. less-resisted elemental in others), pattern (Mighty and Void are PBAoE, Cryonic and Vorpal are cones, Pyronic is a ranged AoE, Ion is chain). For min-maxing, Ion is hard to beat — high damage, very high target cap at upper tiers, and it auto-chains, so you don't need to aim it carefully.

INTERFACE: Adds an inherent proc to all damaging attacks (debuffs, extra DoT, etc.). Most trees offer two effects with two Tier 4 versions weighting them differently. Debuffs stack up to 4 times on a single mob. Reactive's resistance debuff is small (2.5% per stack, max 10% at 4 stacks for 10 seconds), so the Radial Flawless variant with a higher DoT proc chance can be more effective overall.

LORE: Mastermind-style summonable pets that last 3–5 minutes on a 15-minute recharge. They change zones with you. Use the Mastermind pet panel (Aggressive/Defensive/Passive, target attack), but you can't drag Inspirations onto them. Core (left) side focuses on damage; Radial (right) side adds a support pet that's intangible at Tier 4. Take Core on support ATs that don't need extra healing (and on tough ATs like Tankers); take Radial on squishier damage dealers (Brutes, Blasters). Tier 3 and 4 grant an Incarnate Level Shift that applies to Incarnate content only (Trials, Dark Astoria — not Apex/Tin Mage).

DESTINY: Click-to-cast AoE buff auras strongest in the first 30 seconds. Ageless = recharge + endurance reduction (like a wide-area Speed Boost without the run speed). Barrier = defense + resistance bubbles, with higher-tier rezzes. Clarion = strong mez protection (great vs. Romulus's Nictus self-rez, MSRs, Hami mitos). Rebirth = wide-area mega-heal. Incandescence = whole-league Assemble-the-Team that fires instantly, on a 2-minute timer, plus a knockback/stun and heal-effectiveness boost; useful for Keyes disintegration beam pulls, Hami "wall" relocations, Mothership casualty recovery, Justin Augustine TF travel skips, and offensive openers (place the teleport so KB pushes mobs into a corner). I suggest everyone craft at least the 60-Thread Tier 1 Incandescence even if they main another Destiny. Tier 3 and 4 grant the Incarnate Level Shift (Incarnate content only, not Apex/Tin Mage).

HYBRID: Two-in-one — an inherent ability from being slotted plus 0-Endurance timed toggles that don't shut off on mez. Toggles run up to 2 minutes, then 2 minutes recharge. Variants buff damage, regeneration, control, or team support. Some variants pop "WAYLAY!" over controlled enemies. Pair it thematically: Melee on Tankers/Brutes (regen, resist, defense, taunt aura on Radial), Control on Controllers, Support on Defenders.

FIRST STEPS FOR NEW INCARNATES

STEP ONE: Check your build. Use Mids' Reborn: Hero Designer to plan a respec; slot ATOs and Winter Super Pack enhancements (and catalyze them), purples, Overwhelming Force, etc.

STEP TWO: Build your Alpha. Homecoming auto-unlocks the Incarnate system on training to 50 — no Mender Ramiel arc required, though doing the arc unlocks the Alpha slot instantly and awards a Shard (one mission has a tough EB pair, so bring Shivans/P2W pets or a friend, or autocomplete it). Don't buy Incarnate XP with Threads; the slots unlock fast through normal play. The vendor that sold Alpha unlocks for Astral Merits no longer exists.

SHARD-BASED COMPONENTS: You'll get Shard drops along with Thread drops while playing. Enemies must con at least white to roll for Incarnate drops, so doing missions at -1 with blue/green mobs won't help. Big farmable sources: AE fire farms, Mothership Raids (also lets you buy Gr'ai Matter at the Vanguard table), and the level-50 TFs/SFs (Imperious, Lady Grey, Miss Liberty, Lord Recluse) which each grant a Shard-based component reward once every 18 hours. Monsters and AVs seem to have a higher than average drop chance, making nightly triple Hamidon raids productive.

LEVEL SHIFTING: Tier 3 Alpha grants the first Incarnate Level Shift, making you "50+1." Level 50 mobs become blue, but at +0 notoriety the game still gives you level-50 missions, not level-51. The Alpha shift applies to all content when exemplared to 45+. The Lore and Destiny shifts apply to Incarnate content only (Trials, Dark Astoria) — oddly not Apex/Tin Mage.

WEEKLY STRIKE TARGET: Rotating selection of TFs/SFs that awards double Reward Merits, plus a Notice of the Well if you're 50 with Alpha unlocked. One reward per week across all targets — wait until your Alpha is unlocked before running a WST if you're close to 50. Notice of the Well is a key Tier 3 Alpha component; using Shards for the Alpha is partly justified by the fact that you'll already be earning a Notice each week from the WST.

TIER 4 ALPHA — SHARDS VS THREADS: Tier 4 needs a Favor of the Well, which costs 2 Notices + 32 Shards. If you don't have Notices banked, that's at least two weeks of waiting. Crafting the Tier 4 Alpha (and the second Tier 3) with Threads is just as easy as for the other slots and avoids the wait.

STEP THREE: BUILD LORE, DESTINY, AND THE OTHER THREAD-BASED SLOTS first, since their Tier 3 versions grant Incarnate Level Shifts and put you at level 53 — a more even footing with the level 54 mobs in Incarnate content. After those, build out the rest in any order; get everything to Tier 3 before pushing to Tier 4. You can craft Incarnate powers as soon as you have the components — you just can't slot them until the slot is unlocked — so it's fine to bank powers ahead of unlocks.

THREAD-BASED COMPONENT SOURCES — three majors: Incarnate Missions (notably Dark Astoria arcs, soloable in ~15 minutes at minimum difficulty for several components per hour, replayable via Ouroboros — running with a team that hasn't done it yet lets the final mission award the component once per teammate); Incarnate Task Forces (Apex and Tin Mage; you must have Alpha unlocked and slotted or you're penalized 4 levels); and Incarnate Trials.

INCARNATE TRIALS: Leagues of 16–24, usually formed in Pocket D or Ouroboros (watch LFG channel). Disable temp/Day Job powers but not Accolades, and many Super Pack temps still work (charges of Restore are still available). Easiest entry-level trials are Lambda (LAM), Behavioral Adjustment Facility (BAF), and Keyes Reactor; BAF is run most often. Avoid "speed LAMs" if you're new — skipping the second temp set increases the chance of failure. Keyes is more complex (don't damage Antimatter until the right phase); review the trial on ParagonWiki and watch whether the leader explains things and has a plan. Magisterium requires Lore and Destiny unlocked but offers higher Rare/Very Rare drop odds. Trial completion gives a component choice (Common most likely, Very Rare hopeful) plus Astral Merits and an Empyrean. Master Of and optional badges add components and Astrals.

FURTHER PROGRESSION: Save Threads and Empyreans — every Rare from Empyreans saves 8 Empyreans, every Very Rare saves 30. After Vet 11 you no longer get Thread floods from leveling, and Empyreans slow as you near Vet 99. Remember each Tier 4 requires building Tier 1–3 twice. Once your character is fully built, convert spare Empyreans to Transcendent Merits (50:1) and email them to alts to give them an Incarnate head-start.
