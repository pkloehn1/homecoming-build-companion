## Summary

- Set bonuses follow you when you exemplar only if the slotted IO's level is at most 3 levels above your exemped level (a per-IO check), and "chance of" / "for 120s" procs stop functioning entirely once you lose the power they're slotted in.
- Attuned IOs effectively scale to your current level, but they cannot scale below the lowest level the set exists at — so an attuned set with a minimum level of 25 stops giving bonuses once you exemp to 21 or lower.
- The OP's three rules: always catalyze/attune orange ATOs and Winter Os (catalyzing literally boosts their stats to "Superior"), never bother catalyzing/attuning purple or PvP sets (their bonuses already work at all levels — boost them instead), and for everything else attune if you exemp and care about set bonuses or boost otherwise.
- Boosters and Catalysts are sold by Merit Vendors and from ATM-style consoles (Generator/Fatman: 5 merits per Booster, 20 per Catalyst); Catalysts also drop very rarely from Incarnate XP at 50, and there is one "first login of the day" catalyst drop per character per day (per Veelectric Boogaloo's reply).
- Materially-additive corrections from replies: there is a known mouse-over display bug on attuned IOs you have outlevelled (they show your level's value but actually only deliver the set's cap-level value); attuned IOs can be "de-attuned"/attuned in place via the Auction House trick; catalyzing only works on Invention Origin set IOs (not Hamidons, not vanilla generic IOs); and you can catalyze an IO already slotted in a power without unslotting it, which saves an Unslotter.

**Background on set bonuses and exemplaring.** Venture opens by establishing how set bonuses behave when you exemp: bonuses (and globals) keep working as long as the level you exemp to is no more than 3 levels below the slotted IO's level, and that check is per-enhancement. He gives the example of a set with five level-25 pieces and one level-40 piece: the sixth bonus drops off once you exemp to level 36 or below. He also notes that "Chance of" and "for 120s" pieces stop working entirely if you lose the power they're slotted in.

**How attuned pieces behave.** Attuned IOs effectively follow your level, "but they have a minimum — they can't follow your level down below where they exist." So an attuned set whose lowest available level is 25 will stop giving bonuses once you exemp to 21 or lower.
