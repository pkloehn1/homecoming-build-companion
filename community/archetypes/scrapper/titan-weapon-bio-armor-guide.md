# Titan Weapon/Bio Armour Guide

## Summary

- A Titan Weapons / Bio Armor Scrapper guide aimed at +4×8 solo content. The author argues that, despite Bio's lowest-tier defense, resistance, and absorb on a Scrapper, TW's burst damage and constant knockdown plus Bio's Absorb shields combine into a build that can routinely solo +4×8 with the Soul Mastery Patron pool (specifically Shadow Meld) for emergency softcapping.
- Power picks: TW's Crushing Blow, Follow Through, Rend Armour, Whirling Smash, and Arc of Destruction are core; Defensive Sweep and Titan Sweep are useful for levelling but skippable on the final build. Every Bio Armor power is taken; Genetic Contamination is slotted not for damage but for the Avalanche knockdown proc and set bonuses. Soul Mastery (Moonbeam, Shadow Meld) is preferred for the no-crash 32% defence panic button that lets you softcap against +4 enemies.
- Slotting priorities: Superior Scrapper's Strike in Crushing Blow (crit set bonus), Superior Blistering Cold in Follow Through, Hecatomb (5-slot, no pure-damage 6th piece) in Rend Armour, Armageddon in Whirling Smash, and Obliteration / Avalanche in cones; Steadfast and Gladiator's Armor +Def uniques in resist powers; Aegis in Hardened Carapace for Smash/Lethal defence; Shield Wall (4-slot incl. +5% res) plus a LotG +Recharge in Environmental Modifications; Numina's Convalescence in Ablative Carapace for the ranged defence bonus; Panacea +HP/+End procs in Inexhaustible.
- Momentum is essential: Titan Weapons' first attack is slow and clunky, but each attack grants 10 seconds of accelerated animation. Build chains so attacks recharge in order. Levelling-pace recommendation: at level 23 drop to 0×8, scale up rep as you become secure; herd enemies into cone-cap groups and use inspirations for early defence.
- Survival philosophy: Bio's Absorb shields cap at base HP and cannot be enhanced past that, so Parasitic Aura, Ablative Carapace, and the chained Absorb set keep you full while incoming hits chip. Cascade defence failure and Hamidon-style direct-HP attacks are Bio's main weaknesses; KD on most TW attacks acts like temporary 100% defence ("they can't fight on their backs") and pairs well with the Force Feedback +Recharge proc. Envenomed Daggers (300 stockpiled) cover -regen for AVs and GMs since Reactive's -res debuff is small and Interface -regen is "naff."

### Post 1 — Section: Overview, Archetype Comparison, Power Overview, Pool & Patron Choices, Final Build Slotting, Final Thoughts (2020-01-10, edited 2020-01-29)

The OP introduces this as a personal exploratory analysis of TW/Bio rather than a definitive build, asking readers to be kind on a first guide. Walks through archetype options first (Tank, Brute, Scrapper — Stalker doesn't get TW): a Tank with TW/Bio gets the best survivability and TW's KD reinforces taunting and defence; a Brute trades some Tank survival for higher sustainable damage, but the damage cap can only be touched briefly without dedicated support; a Scrapper has the worst defence/resist/absorb but the highest immediate damage and the chance to crit, and the author's playstyle ("jumping in and taking out the biggest guy before he knows I'm in the room") favours the Scrapper. The remaining power info applies to all three but is written from the Scrapper's perspective.

Archetype-specific enhancements: for the Tank, the AT-specific attuned set's +Resistance proc is great but the +Absorb proc is wasted on Bio because Bio already runs its own large Absorb shield with a low ceiling. For the Scrapper, the AT-specific procs reinforce critical play and pair naturally with TW's high per-hit damage.

**Primary: Titan Weapons.** Power-by-power notes (recharge in seconds; the author's take on whether to take it on a final build):

- T1 Defensive Sweep — light cone (120°), grants 11.25% melee defence per hit (15% on Tanks, plus -Res). 4s recharge. _Handy while levelling on a Scrapper, skippable on final build._
- T2 Crushing Blow — high single-target damage. _Take._
- T3 Titan Sweep — wide PBAoE for early levels. _Levelling pick; skippable on final._
- T4 Build Up — _Take every time._
- T5 Follow Through — single-target, fast under momentum, takes Superior Blistering Cold's stun proc. _Take._
- T6 Taunt — ranged foe taunt. _Skippable on a Scrapper._
- T7 Rend Armour — extreme single-target damage with -Def and -Res, can carry an additional -res proc. Boss killer. _Take every time._
- T8 Whirling Smash — PBAoE with the widest 15' radius in the game and KD; Build Up + a crit on this can clear minions instantly. _Take every time._
- T9 Arc of Destruction — superior cone (120°) with KD. _Take every time._

**Secondary: Bio Armor.** Treat every power as needed; the rest of the guide focuses on slotting since power picks are forced. Hardened Carapace 4-slot Aegis (Smash/Lethal resistance and the +Defence boost) plus the Steadfast and Gladiator's Armor +Def uniques for ~6% free defence to all. Inexhaustible: 2-slot Panacea (the +HP/+End proc plus the heal IO) plus a Performance Shifter +End — the +HP proc passively gives ~64 health every 3–4 seconds; the heal IO bumps Max HP. Environmental Modifications: 4-slot Shield Wall including the +5% resistance unique, with a LotG +7.5% Recharge in the 5th slot — Shield Wall outperforms Luck of the Gambler at the bonus level if you don't need accuracy. Evolving Armour: 2-slot Unbreakable Guard for +7.5% Max HP. Ablative Carapace: Numina's Convalescence for the ranged-defence tail bonus (only recharge and heal matter inside the power, since absorb caps to base HP). Genetic Contamination: slot for set bonuses and the knockdown proc, not damage — its ticks are ~30 to red enemies but knockdown is "100% defence for 2 seconds." Parasitic Aura: caps absorb at ~1300 already, so heal slotting is wasted; lean on recharge.

**Power pools.** Speed → Hasten (constant, not strictly required but lets you go full speed). Leaping → Combat Jumping for a defence top-up. Leadership → Maneuvers and Tactics (Tactics' to-hit reduces your accuracy needs and helps against fear/blind). The Patron Pool is personal — the author chose Soul Mastery so the build can sit at +4×8 against any enemy type:

- **Moonbeam** — long-range snipe, usable as a regular ranged shot in combat or as a sniper attack out of combat; great for runners or separating multiple AVs.
- **Shadow Meld** — the headline reason for the choice. Functions like Elude with no crash: 15s duration, 25s recharge, +32% defence to everything on activation, allowing soft-cap against Incarnate-level enemies and giving headroom against -Def hits.

**Final build attack chains.** Two chains, an AoE chain and a single-target chain. Pick the biggest target in the mob, use the AoE chain, then switch to single-target to finish bosses.

Single-Target slotting:

- Crushing Blow — 6× Superior Scrapper's Strike (attuned) for the crit set bonus.
- Follow Through — 6× Superior Blistering Cold (attuned) for defence bonuses and the fast hold proc.
- Rend Armour — 5× Hecatomb (purple); skip the pure-damage 6th since it's wasted with diminishing returns.

AoE slotting includes Armageddon in Whirling Smash and Obliteration / Avalanche / Scirocco's-style cone slotting in Arc of Destruction (per later edits and follow-ups). Arc of Destruction is where the per-hit crit proc fires reliably because the cone usually catches 5 targets, generating enough triggers to fuel Whirling Smash + Rend before momentum drops.

**Mids Reborn build link is provided in the OP** (cohplanner.com download URL) — see Build Attachments below.

**Final thoughts — Momentum.** TW's Momentum mechanic is a 10-second buff that activates the moment you land any TW attack; during that window animation times are dramatically faster. Mids' raw cast times don't reflect this, so the set looks slow on paper. At low level the first attack feels punishing, you must build chains where attacks recharge in order, and endurance is heavy. Once at slot-rich levels with good recharge, Momentum is effectively up all the time inside a fight.

The author closes with a warning: this is sometimes called a "God-tier" build, but it falls over the moment you stop paying attention or hit lag. 20 missions at +4×8 may pass with no real damage taken, then a few seconds of lag costs you the run. "Nothing is immortal and your play style will massively impact how this build works for you." A January 29 edit adds that the build will happily run +2×8 on basic level 25 IOs, so the high-priced final loadout is a destination rather than a prerequisite.

### Post 8 — Section: Build Critique Response, Slotting Tweaks (2020-06-15)

Going through a reader's alternate build:

- Crushing Blow slotting is correct.
- Hardened Carapace: prefers Aegis for the Defence boost over flat resistance.
- Inexhaustible: a whole set is overkill — its real value is +Max HP, and the regen/recovery boost makes it a proc-host candidate, not a 6-slot.
- Skips Titan Sweep — by 32 you have more AoE than you'll ever need.
- Environmental Modifications looks neglected in alternate builds; it's the primary defence toggle and Bio works best on defence/absorb.
- Follow Through is single-target, so Force Feedback +Recharge will rarely fire there — better to slot Superior Blistering Cold for the heavy-hit, stun, and hold.
- Build Up needs love: 2 recharge IOs put it at ~20s with set bonuses, so it's up every encounter.
- Ablative Carapace: prefer Numina's Convalescence for the ranged-defence bonus tail; only recharge and healing matter inside the power.
- Weave wants more slots if you're playing defence/absorb.
- Tough: Aegis again for the defence boost (resistance alone won't save a Scrapper).
- DNA Siphon: not necessary as a damage power; its damage is in the Boxing range. Two recharge IOs as a panic button is enough.
- Whirling Smash purple set is great, but slot the 6th piece despite "Rule of 5" because the unique adds value over the omitted pure-damage IO.

### Post 9 — Section: Replies on Cardiac vs. Musculature, Adaptation, LotG Returns, Fitness (2020-06-19)

- Cardiac (T3) was suggested as an _option_ early on because it's easier to obtain than Destiny and reduces endurance pressure; the author moved straight to Musculature once Destiny was up.
- Runs only Offensive Adaptation — swapping adaptations mid-fight cost more than the benefit.

### Post 10 — Section: Critique of Pylon-Test Comparisons (2020-06-20)

Argues that pylon tests are pure damage tests, not gameplay/survival tests — a pole that can't move is a punching bag. Comparing the alternate build, the author points out that across-the-board resistance is lower in the comparison build, with sacrificed defence to _all_ damage types in order to push S/L defence to 45%; with no defence resistance, one lucky shot on a debuff and that 45% goes poof. Then the author lists places where the comparison build runs into diminishing returns: Crushing Blow at 182% damage-bonus down to 132% (50% wasted), Inexhaustible heal at 145% down to 101%, Follow Through 160% down to 130%, Ablative Carapace 145% down to 101%, Rend Armour over 200% damage clipped to 138%, Whirling Smash 227% to 140% (omitting fire-damage proc), Arc of Destruction with damage and recharge both ED-capped. The takeaway: stat tables read green, but a ton of enhancement value is being burned to satisfy a pylon-shaped problem.

### Post 11 — Section: Elemental-Damage Coverage and Shadow Meld Tactics (2020-06-24)

Fire/Cold should be treated as elemental damage, not just farm damage — Council, Crey, Praetorian Clockwork, Banished Pantheon, Malta missiles, and the Winter event all push fire/cold as primary or secondary effects. Aegis gives meaningful defence across that exotic damage. Shadow Meld is used while _running in_, often mid-air, so the author lands in the centre of the mob with 15 seconds to kill 2–3 purple bosses and the entire mob untouched. It also provides enough buffer to ride through Nemesis Vengeance buffs.

### Post 12 — Section: Speed of Sound, Jaunt, Shadow Meld, -Regen, Absorb Cap (2020-07-23)

Speed of Sound is the author's preferred travel; jaunt teleports between nearby mobs and behind ITF surgeons, and the player can queue an attack so they materialize already swinging — animation time is the build's biggest weakness, anything that cuts it down is great. Shadow Meld is "100% needed if you like running +4×8 ignoring the enemy type" — soloed Ghost Widow at level 54 in under a minute with no hit landing. -Regen comes from the Incarnate Interface that adds it to every attack plus 300 stockpiled Envenomed Daggers; most AVs and GMs go down without daggers but a few regen too fast otherwise. Absorb is capped at base HP, which is why Parasitic Aura and Ablative Carapace don't need heal slotting — they're both already at 1300.

### Post 19 — Section: Overkill, Defensive Sweep, Sample Chains, Crushing Blow Use Case (2020-08-21)

Hits seldom leave a sliver. With overkill, level-54 minions and lieutenants are 2-shots and bosses are 3-shots unless they have heals or innate resists (e.g. Behemoths casting Healing Flames at 30%). Defensive Sweep with the 5-target hit-cap looks great early because you need the AoE and Defence (same with Titan Sweep). Once Arc of Destruction is unlocked it's a superior version of Defensive Sweep, acting as either a single-target or AoE combo builder.

Sample chain: Arc of Destruction → Whirling Smash → Rend Armour → Follow Through → Crushing Blow. By the end, all minions and lieutenants are dead, the targeted boss is dead on Follow Through (or on Rend Armour if it crits — which often happens because of AoD's crit chance, in which case Follow Through transitions to the second boss). At that point AoD is recharged, often with Build Up, for another cycle: Arc of Destruction → Rend Armour → Follow Through. Crushing Blow is only really needed if Whirling Smash isn't required (single-target work like a GM/AV: AoD → Rend → Follow Through → Crushing Blow). At level 32, AoE from Defensive Sweep / Titan Sweep is no longer needed; Crushing Blow isn't critical except as a chain filler when soloing AVs, but it's a good slotting host because the chain wants the filler.

### Post 20 — Section: AoD Crit Procs, Build-Up as Alpha Tool (2020-11-30)

Build Up is an alpha-strike tool, not a "let it tick" tool — you pick when to fire it. With a completed build, Build Up is up every ~15 seconds in combat, and almost always procs solo. The build was designed to take out all but the strongest enemies without outside tools (Reichsman needed Envenomed Daggers for the HP pool; Romulus is faster with daggers but tractable without). **Note:** Hamidon's attacks bypass absorption and target HP directly, which makes Bio look weak there — it's the encounter design, not the build.

### Post 21 — Section: WindDemon's "FYI" Recharge-Cap Reply (2022-03-02)

The recharge cap for Build Up is 1/5 of base recharge, so the absolute minimum is 18s; defence-balanced Bio builds usually land near 21s with a perma-rech proc rather than at the floor. The author concedes the math but counters that they never wait on Build Up in actual play — running into a mob, popping the T9 secondary for the Absorb shield, targeting the biggest enemy, hitting Build Up, then chaining AoE / single-target / AoE before the buff drops kills every minion, most lieutenants, and the first boss with the second ready to fall.

### Post 22 — Section: Levelling Advice, Holiday Server, Bio Pace (2022-05-24, edited)

TW has devastating recharge and animation times; Titan Sweep is an early-game must for clearing until the full chain has recharge reduction. Bio Armor is "take it when needed" — Hasten early helps but don't rush it if you don't feel safe. For mission rep, the build is mostly AoE so XP is best at -1 difficulty but ×8 size; at level 23 move to 0×8 and step up rep as you become secure. Herd enemies into cone-cap groups in early levels and use inspirations to bolster defence; later powers replace that crutch.

### Post 23 — Section: TW-Nerf Era Reassessment (2024-05-05)

The nerf "didn't apply much" to this build. The biggest impact was the range reduction on the PBAoE. Recently ran +4×8 random radio missions in PI; new enemy power adjustments were more of an issue than the build itself. Malta felt tougher and had new powers; Council had small tweaks. Other min/max builds may pull 5–10% more on 1–2 stats but at the cost of the build's overall consistency. The build is the same against any enemy at any level from Ouroboros up.

### Post 24 — Section: Defence Math, Cost, Recharge Priority (2024-05-05)

The build hasn't materially changed; the Mids designer file is still accurate. It relies on 35–38% defence to everything as its baseline; since it kills most groups in 3–5 seconds there isn't much retaliation. Against debuffing enemies, Shadow Meld pushes defence into the 70s, making you essentially untouchable for the duration. By the time it wears off, the absorb shield is fully charged and most things are dead. Recharge is high priority — it gives perma-Hasten and lets the absorb shield be re-applied almost continuously. Cost: about 1 billion to build at the time, but the author used merits, seasonal events, and a substantial alt-stockpile to lower the actual outlay.

### Post 25 — Section: Survival Triad and Build's Place in It (2024-05-16)

Survival has three pillars:

- **Regeneration** — heal faster than you're hit; hard to do at +4×8 without serious recharge.
- **Resistance** — soak damage at up to 75% mitigation, four times longer survival, pairs with regen.
- **Defence** — don't get hit at all; soft-cap (45%) is the goal, hard-cap is essentially debuff insurance reachable only with inspirations.

Bio is a blend of all three: idle near the soft-cap, in combat ~70% defence to everything (taking ~5% of incoming damage in an ideal world), with absorb to soak the unlucky hits. Like the Fire/Kin Controllers of old, it doesn't pay until you're in deep — all the buffs need bodies, dead or alive. Run in, pop the T9 Bio, soak the absorb shield, then whale on them. There's a second Absorb shield available, plus a PBAoE heal that works on living and dead targets. TW's KD is everywhere; "every time they're knocked over you have 100% defence for about 2 seconds because they can't fight on their backs," and KD pairs naturally with the Force Feedback +Recharge proc — the more you attack, the faster powers come back. Notable enemies that overpower this loop: Canaries, PPD, and Banished Pantheon when several masks toggle you simultaneously.

### Post 26 — Section: Absorb Mechanics Clarification, HP Cap Edge Cases (2024-05-16)

Absorb shields can't be regenerated over time, only restarted — a 1500 shield against a 2000 hit lets 500 through. The Scrapper HP cap is also easy to bump into; Incarnate level was already near the cap on this build. Side note: not all newer enemies recognise the absorb shield — Hamidon kills you and leaves the absorb at 100%, and the giant walker in the level-40 TF reduces Max HP by a percentage (the author once had 100% health bar but 1 HP).

### Posts 27–34 — Page 5 Q&A Round (2024-05-17 → 2024-05-21)

Shadow Meld as a "panic" button is misnamed — the 3-second activation makes it a _pre-emptive_ button. Pop it on the way in, sometimes mid-air, so activation finishes when you arrive; in a tough fight, fire it twice if aggro is streaming. Don't put it on auto, or it will fire when you don't want it. Bio's main weakness is cascade defence failure — being hit by 20 enemies a second means roughly one will land each second, and with -Def riders that hit chips your defence and snowballs. Disagrees with proc-bombing DNA Siphon for the author's playstyle (preferring predictable damage so chained hits can be planned) but acknowledges it works for proc-focused players. Battle Axe is similar to TW but the author recalls it's pure-Lethal damage. Pandora's Box flashback is the preferred farm: ~90 minutes for 5 levels and 20m+ inf in kills alone. The Reactive resist debuff at the Interface is small (~2.5% per hit) but worth it because TW's slow chain only stacks a few hits per target — 2.5% × 1500 damage is ~45 extra damage per application. Destiny and Judgement see the most use; Hybrid happens to amplify Judgement. Alpha is Musculature (heavy damage bonus). Lore is Banished Pantheon for the immortal pet's PBAoE clearing minions. Posts close confirming the OP was on Excelsior server and welcoming UK-time team-ups.
