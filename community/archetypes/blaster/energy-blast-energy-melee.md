## Summary

- A philosophy-plus-build guide for an Energy Blast / Energy Manipulation
  Blaster nicknamed "Powerbolt." The author's central thesis is "Damage or
  Die" — the Blaster's primary mitigation is killing things before they fire
  back, with near-soft-capped S/L and Ranged defense as a second layer.
- Energy primary picks: drop Power Bolt (T1) and Power Push (Power Thrust
  covers the role), take everything else. Power Blast / Power Burst / Sniper
  Blast as the single-target rotation (insta-snipe with one teammate's
  Tactics or a Kismet); Energy Torrent / Explosive Blast / Nova as AoE, with
  Sudden Acceleration in Nova and Overwhelming Force in Explosive Blast as
  the KB→KD procs to avoid scattering mobs.
- Energy secondary (2019 build): only the first five — Power Thrust, Energy
  Punch, Bone Smasher, Build Up, Energize. Total Focus, Stun, Power Boost,
  and Boost Range are skipped as too slow or low-value for a team-focused
  damage Blaster. Energize goes perma with a single recharge IO.
- Pool / epic picks: Hasten + Combat Jumping + Super Speed + Super Jump
  (SS plus a Stealth IO gives mission invisibility); Leadership
  (Maneuvers/Assault/Tactics) for damage, defense and the snipe threshold;
  Fighting (Tough/Weave) as a proc holder and LotG mule; Mace Mastery
  (Scorpion Shield) — flipped villainside in about 20 minutes — for a +Def
  shield in one pick.
- The 2022 update (Post 3) revises the build for post-launch game changes:
  Tough is reduced to just the two +3% Def uniques and run untoggled; Energy
  Punch is swapped for Total Focus after the animation buff; Energy Torrent
  moves to Artillery for accuracy and ranged def; Nova is sub-optimally
  slotted for damage and made up for with T4 Musculature; Hasten drops to two
  Recharge IOs and Energize goes on auto; Super Jump and Super Speed are
  double-slotted with Blessing of the Zephyr for ranged def; Force Feedback
  +Recharge procs go into Torrent and Sniper Blast. Final defense: 45% S/L,
  43% Ranged.

## Verbatim excerpt

### Post 1 — Section: Philosophy, Primary, Secondary, Ancillaries, Slotting (Harlequin565, 31 May 2019)

The author opens by saying Blasters require a philosophy more than any other
AT, that other ATs envy them, and that a well-built Blaster will be "dusting
yourself off" while teammates are popping greens. They warn the reader
bluntly: "You will die. You will get complaints." Energy/Energy is described
as "even worse" because melee ATs complain about chasing knocked-back
targets and Defenders complain about anchors and AoE debuffs being bumped.
The author's recommended counter is to "Damage or Die," to find a good Storm
defender to duo with, and on PuGs to ease up on Nova/Explosive Blast and
instead use Torrent to KB enemies _back_ into the team — "serving them up to
the fatty tanks on a platter."

**Energy primary.** "Everything here is awesome. But you can't have all the
powers" — the author drops Power Bolt (T1) and Power Push (Power Thrust covers
it) and takes everything else. Power Blast, Power Burst and Sniper Blast are
the single-target rotation; yellow inspirations make the snipe insta-cast,
and one teammate with Tactics also gets the build over the to-hit threshold
for permanent insta-snipe (the build below isn't quite there even with a
Kismet). AoE is "amazing, which also means that the agro is too." Torrent is
described as an early-game lifesaver because its directional KB lets you
punt mobs out of the fight or aim them toward the team, often buying time
for a non-instant snipe to drop a nasty Lt. Explosive Blast plus Nova will
scatter mobs, which is why those two get the unique KB→KD procs (Overwhelming
Force in Explosive Blast, Sudden Acceleration in Nova). The author cautions
that those uniques may not be available immediately, so be wary of annoying
team leaders in the meantime — but quips that minions can't kick you.

**Energy secondary.** The author only takes the first five powers and
explains why. The premise is "death on 2 legs" — no farting about with stuns
or mitigation, just damage. Energy Punch and Bone Smasher are quick
high-damage melee for anything that gets in your face, with the occasional
stun proc to let you back up and resume ranged work. Melee for En/En is
described as somewhere you visit briefly to use Nova, then GTFO. Total
Focus is rejected for its slow animation; Stun is dismissed as a waste
because it doesn't kill anything; Power Boost and Boost Range are also
called "total waste of space" though there's room in the build to add them.
The author tested Power Boost + Power Thrust for "Hellion pinball in Atlas"
but says "the bubblers win at that game." Energize is praised as much better
than the old Conserve Power, with one Recharge IO making it perma in this
build.

**Ancillary / pool picks.** Hasten — "You need speed" — runs on auto for
attack-chain density and Energize uptime, but the author notes you need to
remember to click it manually. Combat Jumping for mobility, a defense scrap
and a LotG mule. Travel: SS + SJ together because Flight wouldn't fit (the
only thematic regret); SS plus a Stealth IO gives effective in-mission
invisibility, often letting the author skip ahead and nuke the boss group
with Aim + Build Up + Nova + Explosive Blast (plus Judgement "for seasoning")
without waiting for stragglers. Leadership: Assault for damage, Tactics for
the snipe threshold, Maneuvers for defense and another LotG. Fighting: Tough
as a proc holder, Weave for defense and a LotG. Mace Mastery: the author
rolled villainside (much easier on Homecoming) for the +Def shield in one
pick — "I think it took me 20 minutes to do the arc."

**Slotting and IOs.** This is described as a cheap build relative to the
author's controller. Overall goals are recharge and defense. Primary
mitigation is killing everything before it returns fire, but with near
soft-capped defense to S/L (common on most attacks and most melee), and to
Ranged (the highest-def category fire bolts and ice attacks will hit
against), the build is resilient. A single purple inspiration or one
teammate with Maneuvers pushes it over the soft cap. Mez is covered by
Clarion (Incarnate) and break frees; Overwhelming Force gives KB protection.
Final defense numbers: 43% S/L and Ranged, 50% Energy, plus a little S/L and
Toxic resist. The expensive pieces are the two catalyzed Superior Blaster
ATO sets (12 × 100 merits), the two unique +3% Def IOs, and the two purples
(2 × 100 merits); LotGs and Kinetic Combats were "not so bad," and Mako was
surprisingly cheap. Overwhelming Force came from grinding the Summer Event
and selling duplicates, which also funded the rest. The author advises _not_
to use Enhancement Converters to fill out a Summer Event set if you only
need one piece — just keep running the event and selling the surplus. The
downside called out: roughly seven toggles, so an in-battle rez may not get
you back fighting quickly unless you go "naked," because the Leadership
toggles are slow.

### Post 3 — Section: 2022 Update — Rationale (Harlequin565, 16 May 2022)

The author returns nearly three years later, prompted by a Reddit thread, and
lists eight rationale changes versus the original 2019 build:

1. Tough is dropped to just the two +Def uniques and is run with the toggle
   off. Whenever they were picking up enough aggro for Tough to matter, they
   were already in trouble that Tough wouldn't save them from.
2. Total Focus is picked up as a swap-out for Energy Punch — the post-launch
   animation changes made it viable, and it hits very hard, serving as a boss
   killer or a "GTFO me now!" button.
3. Energy Torrent runs Artillery (a then-new set) for a bit more accuracy and
   ranged defense.
4. Nova is sub-optimally slotted for damage, but T4 Musculature pushes it back
   up.
5. Hasten drops to two (50+5) slots. Energize goes on auto because the author
   kept forgetting to click it on cooldown; Hasten gets clicked manually
   whenever it's up.
6. Super Jump and Super Speed are each double-slotted with Blessing of the
   Zephyr for ranged defense.
7. Two Force Feedback +Recharge procs go into Energy Torrent and Sniper Blast.
   KB is less of a problem in iTrials because almost everything is resistant
   to it.
8. Slots pulled out of Stamina and Health — they weren't needed. Health is
   "mostly fine, or at 0" and when in deep trouble, green inspirations are
   the lifesaver, not regen or Blaster resistance/regen.

Slottings have been shuffled to accommodate the changes; the author says it
plays "really smooth." Nova, Judgement, and Explosive Blast are all in the
main attack chain. Power Burst and Sniper Blast are the single-target
rotation, but with Incarnate powers flying around the author says getting
into melee is viable, so the two melee attacks get sprinkled in for fun.
Final defense: 45% S/L and 43% Ranged. Dropping the +Recharge IO in Torrent
for a 6th Artillery slot would soft-cap Ranged too, but the author says they
don't really need it given Incarnate uptime.

## Notable replies

The thread's reply discussion is mostly KB-philosophy banter (the unofficial
"Knockback Bureau" running joke) rather than build-additive content, but two
replies offer mechanically useful corrections to the author's "I have no idea
how to control the KB from Explosive Blast or Nova" comment in the 6 June
2019 sub-thread:

- As @Novacat noted in reply 20 (7 June 2019), there are two ways to control
  the KB on Nova/Explosive Blast: positioning yourself so terrain (a wall,
  corner, or ceiling) catches the bodies, and slotting KB→KD procs to remove
  the question entirely.
- As @0th Power agreed in reply 21 (7 June 2019), the KB→KD proc is the
  instantaneous, thought-free solution for the nuke.

These reinforce — and slightly extend — the author's own recommendation to
slot Overwhelming Force in Explosive Blast and Sudden Acceleration in Nova.
